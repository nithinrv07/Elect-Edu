The Indian Election Framework

India holds regular elections at three different tiers, ensuring grassroots democracy up to the federal center.

Lok Sabha (General)

Held every 5 years to elect Members of Parliament (MPs) to the lower house. The party or coalition with a majority (272+ seats) forms the Central Government and elects the Prime Minister.

Vidhan Sabha (State)

Held to elect Members of the Legislative Assembly (MLAs) for each state. The majority party forms the State Government and elects the Chief Minister.

Local Bodies

Elections for Municipalities (urban) and Panchayats (rural). These decide Mayors, Corporators, and Sarpanches, directly impacting local civic issues like roads and water.

The Electoral Process

How the Election Commission of India (ECI) orchestrates the entire election cycle.

1. Election Notification & MCC

The ECI announces the schedule. The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) immediately activates, restricting governments from announcing new schemes that could influence voters.

2. Nominations & Scrutiny

Candidates file their nomination papers along with affidavits declaring assets and criminal cases. The Returning Officer scrutinizes them to ensure validity.

3. Campaigning

Parties hold mega rallies, roadshows, and distribute manifestos. Campaigning strictly ends 48 hours before polling begins (the 'silence period').

4. Polling Day

Voters cast their votes using Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs). For national elections, this often happens in multiple phases across different regions to ensure security.

5. Counting & Results

EVMs from all booths are gathered in strong rooms. On counting day, votes are tallied electronically, and the Returning Officer officially declares the winner.

Be Prepared for Voting Day

  • Get your Voter ID (EPIC): Ensure your name is on the Electoral Roll. You can check this online at the NVSP portal or the Voter Helpline App.
  • Find your Polling Booth: Check your specific part number and polling station via your voter slip distributed by Booth Level Officers (BLOs).
  • Valid ID Proof: Bring your EPIC card. If you don't have it, bring an approved alternative ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, Driving License).

EVM (Electronic Voting Machine)

Press the blue button next to your chosen candidate's symbol.

VVPAT Verification

A slip will print behind a glass window displaying your choice for 7 seconds.

NOTA (None of the Above)

If you don't support any candidate, press the pink NOTA button at the bottom.
